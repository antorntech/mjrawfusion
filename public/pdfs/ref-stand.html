Reference Standards (RS)

 A pharmaceutical reference standard is a substance or material that has been thoroughly characterized and established as a standard of identity, purity, potency, and other critical attributes. 

Types of Reference Standard

Primary Reference Standard: Is a highly accurate and rigorously characterized substance or material that serves as the ultimate reference point for quality control, analytical testing, and regulatory compliance. It's a fundamental standard against which other reference materials, secondary standards, and test samples are compared to ensure accuracy and consistency in pharmaceutical analysis. These standards are manufactured and sold by pharmacopeia like USP, EP, BP, JP and IP. 

Secondary Reference Standard: In the pharmaceutical industry, a secondary reference standard, also known as a working reference standard, is a reference material that is calibrated and validated against a primary reference standard. While it is not the highest level of standard like a primary reference standard, a secondary reference standard is still of high quality and accuracy. It is used as a practical tool in routine analytical testing, quality control, and method validation within laboratories.

 

Impurities and Degradation Impurities

Impurity Standards 

Impurity standards in the pharmaceutical industry refer to reference materials or substances that are used to identify, quantify, and characterize impurities present in pharmaceutical products. Impurities are unwanted substances that can be found in pharmaceutical products as a result of the manufacturing process, degradation, or interaction with other materials.

Degradation Impurities

An impurity resulting from a chemical change in the drug substance brought about during manufacture and/or storage of the new drug product by the effect of, for example, light, temperature, pH, Water, or by reaction with an excipient and/or the immediate container closure system.

Working Standards (WS)

It is drug substance or any other Chemical in the pharmaceutical industry is called as working standard or secondary standard or a working reference standard, refers to a reference material that is calibrated against a primary reference standard and is used for routine analytical testing, quality control, and method validation within a laboratory or manufacturing facility. Working standards serve as practical tools for ensuring accuracy and consistency in day-to-day operations.
